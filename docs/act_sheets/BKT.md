# ACT sheet — β-Ketothiolase deficiency (T2 / mitochondrial acetoacetyl-CoA thiolase deficiency) (BKT)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. Episodic ketoacidotic crises are precipitated by intercurrent illness — emergency-glucose protocol prevents most morbidity.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — C5:1 (tiglylcarnitine) elevation; C5-OH may also rise.
2. **Urine organic acids** — 2-methyl-3-hydroxybutyric acid + tiglylglycine + 2-methylacetoacetic acid (request fresh sample — methylacetoacetate is unstable).
3. **ACAT1 molecular analysis** — preferred over biochem alone, which can normalise between crises.
4. **Glucose, ketones, anion gap during illness** — diagnostic episode is the highest-yield biochem opportunity.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Between crises BIOCHEMISTRY MAY BE NORMAL** — re-test during illness if clinical suspicion persists.
2. **HSD17B10 deficiency (2-methyl-3-hydroxybutyric aciduria)** — overlapping urinary marker; X-linked + neurodegeneration; clinically distinct.
3. **SCHAD / MHBD** — overlapping markers.
4. **Ketogenic state in well child** — secondary mild 2-methylacetoacetate.
5. **Sample storage** — methylacetoacetic acid degrades rapidly; freeze immediately.
