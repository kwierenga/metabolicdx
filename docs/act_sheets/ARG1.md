# ACT sheet — Argininaemia (ARG1 deficiency) (ARG1)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. Unlike other UCDs, hyperammonaemia is rare and subtle in infancy; presentation is progressive spastic diplegia. Early diet + pegzilarginase prevents neurological deterioration.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma amino acids** — Arg markedly elevated (often >500 µmol/L); Cit normal.
2. **Plasma ammonia** — usually only mildly elevated in ARG1, unlike other UCDs.
3. **Urine orotic acid** — mild-moderate elevation.
4. **ARG1 molecular analysis** — confirms; founder variants in some populations.
5. **Baseline neurological exam + brain MRI** — establish progression baseline.
6. **Erythrocyte arginase activity** — adjunct when molecular result equivocal.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Lysinuric protein intolerance (LPI)** — low Arg, Lys, Orn; opposite plasma pattern.
2. **TPN with arginine-containing solution** — usually transient and modest.
3. **Hepatic dysfunction of any cause** — secondary mild Arg elevation.
4. **Neonatal Arg elevation can be transient** — reassess at 1 month if borderline.
5. **Pegzilarginase therapy** — biochem normalises rapidly; document pre-treatment values.
