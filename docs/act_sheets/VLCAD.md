# ACT sheet — Very-long-chain acyl-CoA dehydrogenase (VLCAD) deficiency (VLCAD)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. Risk spectrum spans neonatal cardiomyopathy, infantile hepatic and adult-onset myopathic phenotypes — confirm before discharge and stratify by phenotype.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — C14:1 elevated; C14:1/C2 and C14:1/C12 ratios; (C16+C18:1)/C2 long-chain burden ratio.
2. **Urine organic acids during illness** — C6–C14 dicarboxylic aciduria; often normal between crises.
3. **ACADVL molecular analysis** — confirms; phenotype roughly correlates with residual activity.
4. **Echocardiogram + ECG + CK** — establish baseline cardiac and skeletal muscle status.
5. **Lymphocyte / fibroblast VLCAD enzyme assay** — adjunct when molecular result is equivocal.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Late-onset / mild ACADVL variants** — borderline C14:1 may evade NBS thresholds; carriers can also show borderline biochem.
2. **MCT-containing formulas / ketogenic diets** — secondary C8/C10 rise; less effect on C14:1 but alters AC interpretation.
3. **MATERNAL VLCAD** — passive C14:1 in well infant; resolves spontaneously.
4. **Sample drawn during illness** — non-specific transient long-chain AC elevation.
5. **Premature infants** — immature LCFAO produces modest C14:1.
6. **TPN with intralipid infusion** — alters AC profile broadly.
