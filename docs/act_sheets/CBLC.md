# ACT sheet — Combined methylmalonic acidaemia and homocystinuria (cblC/D type) (CBLC)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Multi-organ involvement (haemolytic uraemic syndrome, retinopathy, leukoencephalopathy) develops fast in early-onset disease; hydroxocobalamin starts day 1 of suspicion.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma total homocysteine** — typically markedly elevated (>100 µmol/L).
2. **Plasma + urine methylmalonic acid** — elevated (less than in mut MMA but consistent).
3. **Plasma amino acids** — METHIONINE LOW (key distinguishing feature from CBS deficiency).
4. **Plasma acylcarnitines** — C3 elevated.
5. **MMACHC / MMADHC molecular analysis** — c.271dupA most common Caucasian variant; confirms cblC vs cblD.
6. **Urgent ophthalmology** — pigmentary retinopathy is progressive; baseline ERG.
7. **Hydroxocobalamin challenge (1 mg IM)** — biochem response defines therapy.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **CBS deficiency** — also high Hcy but Met HIGH (not low) and MMA NORMAL.
2. **MTHFR deficiency** — high Hcy, low Met, but MMA NORMAL.
3. **Isolated MMA (mut/cblA/B)** — high MMA but Hcy NORMAL.
4. **Maternal B12 deficiency** — infant has same triad; reversible with maternal repletion.
5. **TCN2 deficiency** — combined MMA + Hcy with normal serum B12.
6. **cblF (LMBRD1) and cblJ (ABCD4)** — same biochem; molecular result distinguishes.
7. **Pre-treatment with hydroxocobalamin** — biochem normalises within days.
