# ACT sheet — Isovaleric acidaemia (IVA)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Acute neonatal form presents with ketoacidotic crisis days 3–7; chronic form presents later but each crisis carries CNS injury risk.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — confirm C5 elevation.
2. **Urine acylglycines** — ISOVALERYLGLYCINE pathognomonic; persistent between crises.
3. **Urine organic acids** — isovalerylglycine, 3-OH-isovaleric, 4-OH-isovaleric (distinguishes from pivalate confounder).
4. **Plasma ammonia, blood gas, glucose** — assess decompensation.
5. **IVD molecular analysis** — c.932C>T (p.A282V) is a common mild allele identified by NBS that is likely clinically benign.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Pivampicillin / pivalate-containing antibiotics** — produce pivaloylcarnitine that elevates C5 with NORMAL urinary isovalerylglycine; the most common false-positive.
2. **2-Methylbutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency (SBCAD / ACADSB)** — also elevates C5; the distinguishing UAG marker is 2-methylbutyrylglycine, NOT isovalerylglycine.
3. **Mild p.A282V variant** — frequently identified on NBS with low isovalerylglycine and usually clinically benign.
4. **TPN with cysteine-deficient solutions** — secondary mild C5 elevation.
5. **Valproate therapy** — secondary C5 elevation.
6. **Sample drawn during illness** — non-specific transient C5 in healthy infants.
