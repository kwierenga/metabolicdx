# ACT sheet — Argininosuccinic aciduria (ASL deficiency) (ASA_AC)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Neonatal hyperammonaemia presents days 2–7; long-term hepatopathy and hypertension require ongoing surveillance even with biochemical control.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma ammonia STAT**.
2. **Plasma amino acids** — argininosuccinic acid (ASA) pathognomonic; Cit elevated; Arg low.
3. **Urine orotic acid** — typically elevated.
4. **ASL molecular analysis** — confirms.
5. **Baseline LFTs and BP** — chronic hepatopathy and hypertension are common despite biochem control.
6. **Echocardiogram and renal function** — cardiovascular and renal complications are part of the long-term phenotype.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **ASA can be falsely low** — anhydroform interconversion during storage; chromatographic conditions and prompt analysis matter.
2. **Citrullinemia type I (ASS1)** — also high Cit but NO ASA on AA panel.
3. **Mild / late-onset cases** — biochem may be near-normal at baseline; require protein challenge to provoke.
4. **TPN with Cit-containing solutions** — secondary Cit elevation without ASA.
5. **Arginine supplementation already started** — alters interpretation of Cit/Arg/ASA balance.
