# ACT sheet — Ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC) deficiency (OTC)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day for symptomatic neonatal males; expedited workup for asymptomatic NBS-flagged females (X-linked, variable expression). Emergency UCD protocol if any encephalopathy.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma ammonia STAT**.
2. **Plasma amino acids** — low Cit, low Arg, elevated Gln (often >1000 µmol/L).
3. **Urine orotic acid** — markedly elevated; the diagnostic marker that distinguishes OTC from CPS1/NAGS (where normal).
4. **OTC molecular analysis** — X-linked; confirms; genetic counselling for heterozygote risk.
5. **Allopurinol orotic acid challenge** — for symptomatic carrier females with normal baseline biochem (orotic aciduria provoked).
6. **N-carbamylglutamate (NCG) trial** — should reverse biochemistry only in NAGS deficiency; if response in CPS1-like presentation, suspect NAGS.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **CPS1 / NAGS deficiency** — same low Cit/Arg pattern but orotic acid NORMAL.
2. **Heterozygous OTC female carriers** — biochem may be intermittent/normal; clinical picture and molecular result key.
3. **Hereditary orotic aciduria (UMPS deficiency)** — also high orotic but with megaloblastic anaemia, NOT hyperammonaemia.
4. **Allopurinol exposure** — produces false-positive orotic aciduria.
5. **Sample after dialysis** — biochem normalises rapidly.
6. **Pyrimidine-pathway disorders** — can produce orotic-aciduria patterns unrelated to urea-cycle function.
