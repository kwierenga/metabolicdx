# ACT sheet — Carnitine palmitoyltransferase II / Carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase (CPT2 / CACT) deficiency (CPT2)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day for neonatal-onset (severe acidosis + cardiomyopathy days 1–3); routine for adult-onset myopathic form. CACT is uniformly severe-neonatal.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — long-chain AC elevated (C16, C18, C18:1); (C16+C18:1)/C2 ratio; C0 normal/low (opposite of CPT1).
2. **Urine organic acids during illness** — dicarboxylic aciduria.
3. **CPT2 / SLC25A20 molecular analysis** — distinguishes CPT2 (myopathic + severe neonatal forms) from CACT (always severe neonatal).
4. **Echocardiogram + ECG + CK**.
5. **For adult myopathic CPT2** — exercise/fasting provocation under controlled conditions; biochem can be normal at rest.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Late-onset CPT2 myopathic adult form** — biochem may be near-normal at rest; provoked by exercise / fasting / illness / cold.
2. **VLCAD deficiency** — overlapping long-chain AC pattern; ACADVL vs CPT2 molecular analysis distinguishes.
3. **MCT-containing formulas** — mask the long-chain pattern.
4. **Premature infants** — immature LCFAO produces modest long-chain AC.
5. **Maternal heterozygosity** — passive AC pattern in well infant.
