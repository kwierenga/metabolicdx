# ACT sheet — Homocystinuria due to cystathionine β-synthase (CBS) deficiency (CBS)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. NBS by free homocystine has high false-negative rate for milder/B6-responsive forms — second-tier plasma total homocysteine is essential.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma total homocysteine (tHcy)** — diagnostic; >50 µmol/L confirms; correlates with severity.
2. **Plasma amino acids** — methionine HIGH (key feature distinguishing CBS from remethylation defects), low cystathionine, low cysteine.
3. **Plasma + urine methylmalonic acid** — must be NORMAL to confirm CBS (rules out cblC/D and isolated MMA).
4. **Plasma B12 + folate + holoTC** — exclude nutritional / cofactor causes.
5. **CBS molecular analysis** — predicts B6-responsiveness (p.I278T responsive; p.G307S non-responsive Celtic founder).
6. **Pyridoxine challenge (200–500 mg/day × 4 weeks)** — B6-responsive if tHcy falls ≥30%; defines treatment arm.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Free homocystine on NBS misses B6-responsive forms** — homocystine appears in urine only above plasma tHcy ~50 µmol/L; second-tier plasma tHcy mandatory.
2. **Remethylation defects (cblC, cblG, MTHFR)** — high Hcy with LOW methionine; opposite Met/Hcy pattern.
3. **B12 / folate deficiency** — secondary hyperhomocysteinaemia; resolves on supplementation.
4. **Sample handling — Hcy in serum/whole blood rises rapidly post-collection** — chill within 15 min and centrifuge promptly or value will be falsely high.
5. **MAT1A deficiency** — isolated hypermethioninaemia with normal/low Hcy.
6. **Hawkinsinuria, GNMT** — high Met without Hcy elevation.
