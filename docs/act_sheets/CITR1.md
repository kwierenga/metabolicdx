# ACT sheet — Citrullinemia type I (ASS1 deficiency) (CITR1)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Neonatal hyperammonaemia in classic form develops days 2–7 — admit for plasma ammonia and amino acids before discharge if NBS shows markedly elevated citrulline.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma ammonia STAT** — >150 µmol/L mandates emergency UCD protocol; >500 µmol/L mandates haemodiafiltration.
2. **Plasma amino acids** — Cit markedly elevated (>500–1000 µmol/L in classic); Arg low; Cit/Arg ratio >4 supports diagnosis (>10 strongly supports).
3. **Plasma argininosuccinic acid** — should be NORMAL (presence indicates ASL deficiency, not ASS1).
4. **Urine orotic acid** — elevated downstream of carbamoyl-phosphate accumulation; distinguishes from CPS1/NAGS where normal.
5. **ASS1 molecular analysis** — confirms; phenotype-genotype correlation moderate.
6. **For mild/late-onset suspected** — protein loading test with serial ammonia + Cit measurement under supervision.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Citrin deficiency (CITR2 / SLC25A13)** — mild-moderate Cit elevation with NICCD (cholestasis, lactosuria, galactosuria); very different management.
2. **Argininosuccinic aciduria (ASL)** — Cit also elevated; ASA on AA panel distinguishes.
3. **Sample after dialysis** — Cit normalises rapidly; document pre-dialysis biochem.
4. **Pyrroline-5-carboxylate synthase (P5CS) deficiency** — low Cit AND low Arg AND low Orn; opposite pattern but on differential of UCD-like presentations.
5. **TPN with Cit-containing solution** — uncommon but described.
6. **Arginine therapy started before sampling** — alters Cit/Arg ratio interpretation.
