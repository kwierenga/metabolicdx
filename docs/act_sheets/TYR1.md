# ACT sheet — Tyrosinaemia type I (hepatorenal tyrosinaemia) (TYR1)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Risk of fulminant liver failure within weeks; nitisinone is dramatically effective and must start before liver injury becomes irreversible.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Whole blood / plasma succinylacetone (SA)** — pathognomonic even at trace levels; the diagnostic biochemical test.
2. **Plasma amino acids** — Tyr typically elevated but NOT a sensitive screen on its own; Met also elevated in severe disease.
3. **Urine organic acids** — succinylacetone, 4-OH-phenylpyruvate, 4-OH-phenyllactate.
4. **LFTs, INR, alpha-fetoprotein (AFP)** — AFP characteristically very high; baseline for hepatocellular carcinoma surveillance.
5. **Renal tubular function (Fanconi panel)** — electrolytes, glucosuria, generalised aminoaciduria, phosphaturia, hypophosphataemic rickets.
6. **FAH molecular analysis** — confirms; Quebec/Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean founder p.E364G (c.1062+5G>A) common.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Tyrosine-only NBS has low sensitivity** — modern programmes use SA on DBS; if Tyr-only is in use, second-tier SA is essential.
2. **TYR2 (Richner-Hanhart, TAT)** — high Tyr (>500 µmol/L) but NO succinylacetone, NO liver disease, oculocutaneous features; benign on dietary control.
3. **TYR3 (HPD)** — modest Tyr elevation, no SA, variable phenotype.
4. **Transient tyrosinaemia of newborn / preterm liver immaturity** — mild Tyr rise without SA; resolves spontaneously.
5. **TPN with hepatic dysfunction** — secondary Tyr/Met/Phe elevation; SA absent.
6. **Sample after nitisinone started** — SA suppressed; document pre-treatment biochem before therapy.
