# ACT sheet — Medium-chain acyl-CoA dehydrogenase (MCAD) deficiency (MCAD)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm and educate the family before the infant's first prolonged fast or intercurrent illness — first crisis can be fatal and is preventable with simple fasting avoidance + emergency-glucose protocol.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — confirm C8 elevation (typically >0.3 µmol/L) and C8/C10 ratio (>2, often >5 in classic disease).
2. **Urine acylglycines — hexanoylglycine and suberylglycine** — more specific than urine organic acids; the confirmatory biochemical test even between crises.
3. **ACADM molecular analysis** — c.985A>G (p.K329E) homozygosity in ~80% of Northern European patients; compound heterozygotes with c.199T>C / p.Y67H or other mild alleles have attenuated biochemistry and may evade NBS.
4. **Plasma free + total carnitine** — often low; informs whether carnitine supplementation is needed.
5. **Urine organic acids** — only diagnostic during acute illness (dicarboxylic aciduria with inappropriately low ketones); a normal between-crisis sample does NOT exclude MCAD.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **MCT-containing formula (premature infant formula, ketogenic regimens)** — produces marked C8 elevation that mimics MCAD; check feeding history before workup.
2. **Valproate therapy** — secondary C6/C8/C10 elevation on AC.
3. **Mild ACADM variants (e.g., p.Y67H, p.Y42H)** — borderline DBS C8 may evade NBS thresholds while still carrying meaningful crisis risk; molecular result reframes interpretation.
4. **Heterozygote carriers** — occasional borderline C8 on AC with no biallelic ACADM variants and no clinical risk.
5. **Pivampicillin / pivalate-containing antibiotics** — elevate C5, NOT C8; common confounder when reviewing AC panels but unrelated to MCAD.
6. **Sample collected during acute illness** — transient elevations can occur in healthy infants; repeat when well.
