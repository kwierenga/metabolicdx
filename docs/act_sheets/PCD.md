# ACT sheet — Primary carnitine deficiency (organic cation/carnitine transporter, OCTN2) (PCD)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. Sudden cardiac death and skeletal myopathy are the principal risks; lifelong oral carnitine fully prevents.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma free + total carnitine** — markedly low (typically <10 µmol/L).
2. **Plasma acylcarnitines** — generally low across the board (the 'empty profile'); C0 minimal.
3. **Urinary carnitine + fractional excretion** — INAPPROPRIATELY HIGH given low plasma (renal wasting; the diagnostic signature).
4. **SLC22A5 molecular analysis** — confirms.
5. **MATERNAL plasma carnitine** — NBS may detect MOTHER, not infant; check mother's free carnitine and screen for cardiomyopathy.
6. **Echocardiogram baseline** — cardiomyopathy can be silent and is reversible with carnitine.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **MATERNAL PCD detected by infant NBS** — passively low neonatal C0 reflects maternal disease in ~25–30% of low-C0 NBS positives; identifies an at-risk mother.
2. **Maternal vegan/vegetarian diet** — secondary mild low C0.
3. **Sample collected on day 1** — maternal carnitine still circulating; repeat at 1–2 weeks.
4. **Premature infants** — physiologically low C0.
5. **Pivampicillin / pivalate antibiotics** — deplete carnitine; secondary low C0.
6. **Valproate therapy** — secondary low C0.
7. **Other FAO disorders (mut MMA, PA, MCAD long-term)** — secondary carnitine depletion.
8. **TPN without carnitine supplementation** — easily-overlooked iatrogenic cause.
