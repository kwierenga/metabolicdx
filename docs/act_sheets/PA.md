# ACT sheet — Propionic acidaemia (PA)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day clinical callback. Risk of irreversible neonatal decompensation within the first week — admit and start metabolic emergency protocol pre-emptively if presymptomatic NBS-positive on day 5–7.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma ammonia, lactate, blood gas, anion gap, glucose** — characterise acute decompensation; ammonia >200 µmol/L mandates dialysis planning.
2. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — confirm C3 elevation and C3/C2, C3/(C16+C18:1) ratios; C3/Gly is the most specific single ratio.
3. **Urine organic acids** — methylcitrate (MCA), 3-hydroxypropionate, propionylglycine, tiglylglycine; MCA >> MMA distinguishes from MMA.
4. **Plasma total homocysteine + plasma methionine** — REQUIRED to exclude cblC/cblD (also elevates C3, but with hyperhomocysteinaemia and low Met).
5. **PCCA / PCCB molecular analysis** — confirms diagnosis; clarifies founder variants (Inuit PCCA c.1540+1G>A, Saudi PCCB c.990dupT).
6. **Baseline echocardiogram, ECG, CBC** — establish pre-treatment cardiac and haematological status (cardiomyopathy and cytopenias are core complications).

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Maternal B12 deficiency / pernicious anaemia / vegan diet / gastric bypass** — elevates infant C3 with secondary hyperhomocysteinaemia and methylmalonic aciduria; resolves on maternal repletion. Always ask.
2. **cblC / cblD / cblF / cblJ** — same C3 signature with low Met and high homocysteine; missing these is the commonest interpretive error.
3. **Sample after blood transfusion or post-haemodialysis** — can normalise C3 and mask abnormality; defer second-tier testing 48–72 h.
4. **Premature / sick infants with secondary mitochondrial dysfunction** — modest C3 elevation that resolves with clinical recovery.
5. **Valproate, ECMO, parenteral nutrition with propionate-containing components** — secondary C3 elevation.
