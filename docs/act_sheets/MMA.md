# ACT sheet — Methylmalonic acidaemia (mut / cblA / cblB) (MMA)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Neonatal decompensation similar to PA; B12-responsive forms (cblA/B) have better outcome but only if hydroxocobalamin started early.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma ammonia, lactate, blood gas, anion gap, glucose** — assess decompensation.
2. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — confirm C3 and C3/C2 ratio.
3. **Urine organic acids** — methylmalonic acid (markedly elevated); methylcitrate (less prominent than in PA, the inverse of the PA pattern).
4. **Plasma total homocysteine — REQUIRED** to exclude cblC/D/F/J (combined disorders need different therapy).
5. **Plasma + urine MMA quantitation** — distinguishes MUT (very high) from cblA/B (intermediate) from MCEE / TCN2 / nutritional (mild).
6. **Hydroxocobalamin challenge (1 mg IM × 5 days)** — defines B12-responsiveness; cblA/B respond, mut does not.
7. **MMUT / MMAA / MMAB / MMACHC molecular analysis**.
8. **Baseline renal function + cystatin C** — CKD is the long-term complication.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **cblC / cblD (MMACHC, MMADHC)** — high MMA + high Hcy + low Met; very different therapy (lifelong hydroxocobalamin + betaine).
2. **Maternal B12 deficiency** — infant secondary MMA elevation; ask about maternal vegan diet, gastric bypass, autoimmune gastritis, PPI use.
3. **TCN2 deficiency** — combined MMA + Hcy with NORMAL serum B12 (most B12 is haptocorrin-bound; misleading).
4. **MCEE deficiency** — mild MMA elevation; usually clinically benign.
5. **Acquired B12 / folate deficiency in older child** — reversible with supplementation.
6. **Sample post-transfusion or post-haemodialysis** — masks abnormality; defer testing 48–72 h.
7. **Vitamin B12 pre-treatment** — biochem normalises rapidly; document pre-treatment values.
