# ACT sheet — Carnitine palmitoyltransferase I (CPT1A) deficiency (CPT1)

**Urgency:** URGENT

Confirm within 1–2 weeks. Hypoketotic hypoglycaemia + Reye-like crises occur during fasting; preventable with strict fasting avoidance.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — C0 HIGH; long-chain AC LOW (the inverse pattern of CPT2/CACT).
2. **C0/(C16+C18) ratio** — markedly elevated; the diagnostic ratio for CPT1.
3. **Urine organic acids during illness** — dicarboxylic aciduria.
4. **CPT1A molecular analysis** — confirms; the Arctic founder variant p.P479L (c.1436C>T) is very common in Inuit / First-Nations populations with variable clinical risk.
5. **Glucose + ketones during illness** — confirms hypoketotic hypoglycaemia.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **Arctic p.P479L variant** — extremely common in Inuit/First-Nations populations; phenotype variable; counselling cautiously and prioritising fasting-avoidance education.
2. **MATERNAL CPT1** — passive high infant C0; resolves on independent feeding.
3. **MCT-containing formula** — modest secondary effects on AC profile.
4. **Lab interpretation note** — CPT1 is one of the few FAO disorders with HIGH C0 (most show low C0); ensure NBS lab applies the correct directionality.
5. **Premature infants** — immature LCFAO; C0 can be elevated transiently.
