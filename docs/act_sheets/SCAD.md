# ACT sheet — Short-chain acyl-CoA dehydrogenase (SCAD) deficiency (SCAD)

**Urgency:** ROUTINE

Routine. Current evidence does not consistently link SCAD with disease; many centres no longer treat asymptomatic NBS-positive SCAD. Confirm and document but counsel reassurance.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Repeat plasma acylcarnitines** — C4 elevated.
2. **Urine organic acids** — ethylmalonic acid (EMA) ± methylsuccinic.
3. **Urine acylglycines** — butyrylglycine.
4. **ACADS molecular analysis** — c.625G>A (p.G185S) and c.511C>T (p.R171W) are common low-penetrance variants frequently identified by NBS; clinical significance very limited.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **IBD / ACAD8 deficiency** — also elevates C4; distinguishing UAG marker is isobutyrylglycine (no EMA).
2. **MADD (GA2)** — multi-chain AC pattern includes C4 — broader profile distinguishes.
3. **Riboflavin transporter deficiency (SLC52A2/A3)** — multi-chain AC pattern; MADD-like.
4. **Common ACADS variants are very prevalent in the general population** — biochem may persist with no clinical phenotype; counselling matters.
5. **ETHE1 (ethylmalonic encephalopathy)** — also high EMA but with chronic acidosis, petechiae, severe neurological phenotype; very different disorder.
