# ACT sheet — Maple syrup urine disease (MSUD)

**Urgency:** CRITICAL

Same-day callback. Classic MSUD presents with encephalopathy and cerebral oedema between days 3–7 — admit for plasma BCAA + alloisoleucine quantitation if NBS shows leucine elevation.

## Confirmatory testing

1. **Plasma amino acids** — confirm Leu/Ile/Val elevation; (Leu+Ile+Val)/Ala ratio (low Ala due to anaplerosis block).
2. **Plasma alloisoleucine** — pathognomonic at >5 µmol/L; persists between crises in intermittent forms (only reliable marker in remission).
3. **Urine organic acids** — branched-chain α-ketoacids (2-ketoisocaproic, 2-keto-3-methylvaleric, 2-ketoisovaleric); bedside DNPH positive.
4. **Plasma ammonia, blood gas, lactate, glucose** — assess decompensation.
5. **BCKDHA / BCKDHB / DBT / DLD molecular analysis** — DLD (E3) deficiency adds combined PDH + α-KGDH deficiency with lactic acidosis.
6. **Brain MRI if symptomatic** — characteristic MSUD oedema in dorsal brainstem, cerebellar white matter, globus pallidus, thalami.

## Pitfalls and false-positive causes

1. **TPN with branched-chain enriched solutions / preterm parenteral feeds** — secondary BCAA elevation; repeat off TPN.
2. **Intermittent MSUD between crises** — plasma BCAAs may NORMALISE; alloisoleucine is the only persistent marker.
3. **Premature infants** — modest BCAA elevations; correlate with clinical picture.
4. **Liver failure of any cause** — non-specific BCAA elevation.
5. **Thiamine-responsive MSUD** — milder biochem may evade NBS thresholds; trial 100–300 mg/day after diagnosis.
6. **Sample drawn during catabolism / fasting** in well infant — transient mild BCAA rise that resolves on feeding.
